About Us

The Princeton Undergraduate Research Journal is a biannual publication showcasing the independent work of Princeton undergraduates. Authors submit their research and work with reviewers and editors to prepare articles through the peer review process.

Our goals are to demonstrate the strength of Princeton undergraduate research while allowing undergraduates to acquire skills in academic publishing, an important aspect of the research process.

Additionally, the journal grants students real experience with peer review and academic journal publication and also fosters relationships between students and faculty. Pre-PhD students become stronger candidates for graduate schools and undergraduates are exposed to the Princeton research community to a wider degree through participation with PURJ.
